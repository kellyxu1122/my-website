About Me

With a background in law and a growing passion for technology, I have navigated a career path that bridges legal expertise, product thinking, and data-driven analysis. After earning my Master's degrees in Law, I began my career as a ⚖️ Case Secretary, where I observed how the confidentiality and procedural complexity of online arbitration can make it difficult for parties—especially those unfamiliar with the process—to fully understand their rights and obligations. Knowing there was room for digitization, I decided to apply technology toward improving dispute resolution workflows.

Motivated by that goal, I transitioned into the legal tech industry as a 💻 Legal Product Engineer. I mapped arbitration workflows into prototypes and drafted detailed requirements. By applying regex-based extraction systems, conditional logic, and dependency parsing to analyze judicial documents and collaborating with designers and developers to enhance search and analytics tools, I witnessed how technology greatly improved the predictability of dispute outcomes for all parties—this significantly boosted my sense of accomplishment.

To further develop my technical skills, I joined an 📊 academic research project on spatial–temporal analysis of COVID-19 spread across U.S. counties. I integrated and cleaned case, mobility, and demographic data, then developed regularized regression models to examine how county characteristics influence mobility–transmission dynamics. During that period, relocating abroad with my husband brought considerable challenges, prompting me to reconsider what constitutes universally applicable professional skills and to clarify my learning objectives.

These interdisciplinary experiences and adapting to a new life have motivated me to pursue computer science training. As a mother, I want to achieve meaningful results and set a positive example. I plan to leverage machine learning and large language models for legal text understanding and Q&A, while using microservices and cloud platforms for scalable deployment. My long-term goal is to build an AI-powered legal assistant that compares arbitration rules, estimates costs, and explains procedures—making dispute resolution more transparent and accessible.
